# Google Search Indexing Guide

Your website has been optimized for Google search with all necessary SEO elements. Follow these steps to get your site indexed:

## ‚úÖ What's Already Done

1. **Meta Tags Added:**
   - Title, description, keywords
   - Open Graph tags (for social media)
   - Twitter Card tags
   - Canonical URL

2. **Structured Data Added:**
   - LocalBusiness schema (helps with local search)
   - Organization schema
   - Includes your business information, address, phone, services

3. **Files Created:**
   - `robots.txt` - Tells search engines what to index
   - `sitemap.xml` - Maps all your pages for Google

## üìã Steps to Get Indexed in Google

### Step 1: Verify Your Domain
1. Go to [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console)
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. Click "Add Property"
4. Enter your website URL: `https://www.nssexpress.com` (or your actual domain)
5. Choose a verification method (HTML file upload, HTML tag, DNS, etc.)

### Step 2: Submit Your Sitemap
1. Once verified, go to "Sitemaps" in the left menu
2. Enter: `sitemap.xml`
3. Click "Submit"
4. Google will start crawling your site

### Step 3: Request Indexing (Optional but Recommended)
1. In Google Search Console, go to "URL Inspection"
2. Enter your homepage URL: `https://www.nssexpress.com`
3. Click "Request Indexing"
4. This speeds up the indexing process

### Step 4: Wait for Indexing
- Google typically indexes sites within a few days to a few weeks
- You can check indexing status in Search Console under "Coverage"
- Make sure your site is live and accessible

## üîç Important Notes

1. **Update Domain in Files:**
   - If your domain is NOT `www.nssexpress.com`, you need to update:
     - `sitemap.xml` - Replace all URLs with your actual domain
     - `index.html` - Update canonical URL and structured data URLs
     - `robots.txt` - Update sitemap URL

2. **Keep Content Fresh:**
   - Update your sitemap.xml `lastmod` date when you make changes
   - Google prefers regularly updated content

3. **Monitor in Search Console:**
   - Check for any errors or issues
   - Monitor search performance
   - See which keywords bring traffic
